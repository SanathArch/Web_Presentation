# Case Study: Reunion Island Housing

## 📍 Project Information
- **Location:** Saint-Denis, Reunion Island, France
- **Climate Zone:** Tropical humid
- **Project Type:** Residential housing development
- **Year Completed:** 2024
- **Architect/Firm:** [To be added]

## 🎯 Design Challenge
Design affordable, climate-responsive housing that:
- Withstands cyclone conditions
- Provides thermal comfort without AC
- Respects local building traditions
- Fits within limited budget constraints

## 🏗️ Solutions Implemented

### Passive Cooling
- **Cross-ventilation:** Windows positioned for prevailing trade winds
- **High ceilings:** 3.2m height allows hot air to rise
- **Ventilated roof:** Double-layer roof with air gap

### Ventilation Strategy
- **Central courtyard:** Creates stack effect
- **Louvered windows:** Adjustable for wind direction
- **Roof vents:** Automated, temperature-controlled

### Material Selection
- **Local volcanic stone:** High thermal mass
- **Bamboo accents:** Sustainable, locally sourced
- **Concrete:** Cyclone-resistant structure

### Water Management
- **Rainwater harvesting:** 10,000L storage tanks
- **Greywater recycling:** Garden irrigation
- **Permeable paving:** Reduces runoff

## 💡 Key Learnings

### What Worked Well
- Courtyard design exceeded expectations for airflow
- Residents report 80% reduction in AC use
- Local materials reduced cost by 25%

### What Would We Improve
- Larger overhangs needed for monsoon protection
- Better insect screening for open windows
- More storage for rainwater system
